Playa del Carmen Real Estate Investment Basics

By Richard Houghton
03 October, 2017

Whether you are planning to live in the Mexican Caribbean full time or just want to find a great Playa del Carmen investment property to visit sometimes and rent out, it’s important to understand the basics of buying Mexico real estate. Here’s a quick guide to help you understand more about real estate investment and buying a house in Mexico.

 

 

First, it’s important to point out that Playa del Carmen real estate investment is not for everyone. In fact, buying real estate internationally in any location is not for everyone, which means the decision should never be taken lightly. Regardless of whether you plan to spend all of your time living in the Mexican Caribbean or simply want to have income producing investments in your portfolio, owning property in Playa or anywhere else in Cancun or the Riviera Maya region will have an effect on your life.

This obviously includes a financial impact, but it will also have an effect on your level of familiarity with the local culture and customs. In time, you may even find yourself adopting new habits, patterns of behavior and a different perspective on certain things. Here are a few tips to ensure real estate investing success, regardless of your initial level of knowledge about life in the Mexican Caribbean.

Know Why You Buy

This might sound overly simple, but many buyers don’t really take the time to figure out exactly why they are buying real estate in Mexico, other than a love of the area or a desire to make ongoing investment income. Although both of these reasons are perfectly acceptable, taking the time to define your intent is critical to ensuring that you wind up in the right property. Think about the long term, including whether you will be happy owning property in Playa del Carmen for many years and how much return on investment you need to see in order to feel good about the purchase. Remember: If you’re going to do it, do it right!

Where to Begin?

Regardless of your personal familiarity with the Riviera Maya region, be sure to work with an experienced, reputable local real estate investment advisor who really knows the market in Playa. When searching for a realtor, here are a few things to ask and keep in mind that will help ensure you find someone trustworthy:

  • Only Use a Licensed Realtor
  • Some things really are too good to be true
  • Does the realtor also own property?
  • Does the realtor have an office?
  • Are there previous happy clients? 

Rules of Foreign Property Ownership

First, let’s get one thing straight: Yes, you can own real estate here as a foreigner. Depending on its location – for example, when it’s close to or on the coast – a fideicomiso (real estate trust) may need to be set up to hold the title, which will name you as the owner. Your local property investment advisor should be able to guide you to a reputable Mexican lawyer who is also fluent in your native language to guide you through the process of buying property.

Also, keep in mind that although Mexico uses the peso as its main form of currency, properties are often listed in US dollars in major cities. Make sure you really understand the implications and exchange rates before signing on the dotted line. And finally, when it comes to financing property here, our advice is just don’t. If you don’t have the cash on hand in your personal account, consider buying real estate with an IRA instead.

Other Laws in Mexico

Generally speaking, it’s pointless to involve a lawyer from your home country, since the laws can change and a local attorney will be much better equipped to help you navigate the ins and outs of owning real estate and living full or part time in a foreign country. Don’t be afraid to seek legal advice on everything from the purchase, to immigration rules if you decide to become a full or part-time resident, to finding a great school for your children.
